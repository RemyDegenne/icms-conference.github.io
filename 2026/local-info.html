Local Info

Conference Location

ICMS 2026 will take place in Waterloo Ontario, a city near Toronto. Sessions will be held at the Waterloo campus of Wilfrid Laurier University, with additional events at the University of Waterloo and at the Maplesoft headquarters. All of these are close together in Waterloo, with the universities in walking distance from each other. Transportation will be provided for a reception at Maplesoft.

Travelling to the City of Waterloo

The city of Waterloo is easily reachable by air and ground transportation. The location of the universities is approximately a 25-minute drive away from Waterloo International Airport, 70 minutes from Toronto Pearson International Airport, and 80 minutes from John C. Munro Hamilton International Airport.

Airport Transportation

From the Region of Waterloo International Airport (YKF)
The ICMS 2026 venue is most easily reached by taxi or rideshare. Several local taxi companies operate from the airport, and travel time to campus is approximately 20–30 minutes. Fares vary depending on destination and time of day.

From Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ)
Toronto Pearson International Airport is the region's primary international gateway. The Waterloo area can be reached via:

  • Shared shuttle services (e.g., Airways Transit), which operate 24/7 and provide door-to-door service between Toronto Pearson Airport and the Waterloo region.
  • Car rental, available at the airport.
  • Public transit options, which may involve a combination of train and local transit and typically require more travel time.

Parking and Local Transportation

Parking

Parking is available on the Wilfrid Laurier University (Waterloo) campus. Visitors are encouraged to consult the campus map for building locations and nearby parking areas. Information about parking regulations, rates, and daytime parking options is available on the Wilfrid Laurier University website.


Public Transportation

The Waterloo Region is served by Grand River Transit (GRT), which provides bus and light rail service throughout the region. Wilfrid Laurier University is well served by multiple GRT bus routes along University Avenue West and King Street North. Google Maps can be used to plan trips using GRT services in real time.


Regional and inter-city travel options are also available through GO Transit and VIA Rail Canada, with connections to Toronto and other nearby cities.

Visa Information

To determine whether you require a visa to enter Canada, please consult the Government of Canada’s official resource, "Find out if you need a visa to enter Canada" on the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website. Nearby Canadian embassies and consulates can be found on Foreign Affairs and International Trade Canada's Canadian Offices Abroad page.


If you require a visitor visa to enter Canada and have an accepted paper to present at ICMS 2026, the conference organizers can issue an official invitation letter upon request.


To obtain an invitation letter, please submit the following documentation:

  • A scanned copy of your passport
  • Your institutional postal address
  • Your institutional email address
  • The title of your accepted paper to be presented at ICMS 2026

This documentation is to be sent to Ilias Kotsireas/Stephen Watt with the subject line "Request for ICMS 2026 visa invitation letter". The conference will be unable to process your request for a visa invitation letter if any documentation is missing. If you require an official document, for funding purposes, confirming that you will be travelling to Canada to attend ICMS 2026, please also send the request to Ilias Kotsireas/Stephen Watt. Please ensure the aforementioned matters are addressed as early as possible to allow sufficient time for unforeseen difficulties or delays.

Emergency Information

In case of an emergency requiring police, fire, or ambulance services, dial 911 from any phone in Canada. Campus security services are available 24/7. Several hospitals, urgent care centers, walk-in clinics, and pharmacies are located throughout the Waterloo Region for non-emergency medical needs. During the conference, a designated ICMS 2026 contact will be available to assist participants with emergencies or urgent issues, with contact details provided on-site and in the conference program.
